Image Processing Lab7: Image restoration from different types of noises

Exercise 1: (Practice)
Read the FigP0438(left).tif image. Add to it the Gaussian noise of mean u=10 and standard deviation sd=20. Restore the image using local mean, geometric mean, and harmonic mean. Show the results side by side. (Ignore the boundary pixels and let them remain unchanged, to avoid padding.)

Exercise 2. (Marked)
Read the image noisy(a).tif. Apply the following filters to it:
1. arithmetic mean,
2. geometric mean,
3. harmonic mean,
4. contraharmonic mean filter (use Q=1.5 and Q=-1.5),
5. median filter,
6. alpha-trimmed mean filter (use d=2),
7. min filter,
8. max filter,
9. mid-point filter
Show all results side by side. Which filter performed the best? Based on the results can you guess what type of noise the image had?

Exercise 3:(Marked)
Repeat Ex2 for noisy(b).tif image. Apply all filters and identify the noise from the results.

Exercise 4:(Marked)
Repeat Ex2 for noisy(c).tif image. Apply all filters and identify the noise from the results.

Exercise 5: Exercise 5 (Multiple Noises) (Marked)
Repeat Ex2 for noisy(d).tif image. Apply all filters and identify the noise from the results.

Exercise 6 (Multiple Noises) (Marked)
Read noisy(d).tif image. Apply 1. median filter, 2. alpha-timmed filter (d=4), 3. median filter followed by alpha-trimmed filter, 4. alpha-trimmed filter followed by mean filter Compare results.

Exercise 7 (Optional)(Bonus Mark)
Repeat Ex5 with different combination of filters. Try to find which combination works the best. If you find good results, report to your instructor.
