Image Processing Lab2: Image Basics

Practice

1. Make a 2D matrix A of size 500 x 500. Initialize all elements with 0. Convert B to unsigned 8-bit integers. Display B as an image.

2. Repeat Ex1 but set all pixel intensity values to 255 this time.

3. Change all values of B to 128 and display the image.

4. Change all values of B to random integers in [0,255] range and display the image.

5. Show the results of Exercise 1-4 side by side in a 2x2 table.

6. Generate a 500x500 image with all black pixels and a rectangular 100x100 block of white color in the center.

7. Generate a negative of the image in Ex6, i.e., change all white pixels to black and black to white. Show the original and the negative side by side.

Exercises to be marked

8. Draw a letter T in white color on a 500x500 black background. Choose suitable size of the letter T yourself to look nice.

9. Show a 500x500 image in which every odd-numbered row and column are white, while every even-numbered row and column are black. Do not use the "for" loop or "if" condition in your code. (Can you see the white and black pixels separately? Please zoom in to see them clearly.)

10. Repeat Ex9 using the "for" loop and "if" condition

11. Read MATLAB's sample image spine.tif. Call it A. Calculate its minimum and maximum values and display them. Stretch the image in [0, 255] range (the minimum value should linearly map to zero and the maximum to 255). Call the result as B. Calculate C = B-A. Display A, B, and C side-by-side in a row.

Optional Exercises for Bonus Marks

12. Draw a while circle of radius 100 in the center of a black 500x500 background image.

13. Draw a while disk of radius 100 in the center of a black 500x500 background image.
